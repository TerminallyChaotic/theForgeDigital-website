## Business Structure & Legal

### Connecticut Setup (Lean Start)
- **LLC Formation:** $120 filing fee
- **Annual LLC Report:** $80/year
- **Alternative:** Sole proprietor (cheapest) if you start simple and test the market

### Core Steps
1. Pick structure (LLC recommended for protection)
2. File with Connecticut Secretary of State
3. Get EIN from IRS (free, online)
4. Open business bank account
5. Set up business email on your domain
6. Get business phone number (separate or forwarded)

### Insurance (Budget $500–$1,500/year)
- General liability insurance
- Professional liability / E&O insurance
- Consider inland marine coverage if handling client equipment

## Customer Process

### From Inquiry to Launch
1. **Inquiry received** — Contact form, phone call, or referral
2. **Discovery call** — 30 minutes, understand business, needs, budget
3. **Quote/proposal** — Written estimate with clear scope
4. **Contract signed** — Service agreement, terms, revision limits
5. **Deposit paid** — Usually 50% upfront (adjust as needed)
6. **Project kickoff** — Gather content, set timeline
7. **Revisions** — Typically one or two rounds built in
8. **Launch** — Testing, domain setup, handoff
9. **Maintenance upsell** — Offer care plan immediately
10. **Testimonial request** — Ask for review after launch

### Important Policies
- Define revision limits in contract (typically 1–2 rounds)
- Set deposit percentage (usually 50%)
- Clarify scope boundaries (what's included vs. add-on)
- Hosting and plugins: Either client pays directly or you manage + charge separately
- Emergency support: Define availability and premium fees

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

### Pricing Mistakes
- ❌ Charging $200–$400 for a full website (trains bad clients)
- ❌ Offering unlimited revisions with no limit
- ❌ Including free hosting forever
- ❌ Forgetting that maintenance is your recurring income
- ❌ Competing on price instead of value

### Scope Mistakes
- ❌ Building for "everyone" instead of specific business types
- ❌ Offering too many services at once
- ❌ Over-customizing instead of using repeatable processes
- ❌ Disappearing after launch instead of pushing maintenance plans

### Sales Mistakes
- ❌ Waiting for inbound leads (cold outreach works better)
- ❌ Trying to sound like a big agency
- ❌ Not collecting testimonials
- ❌ Not asking for referrals
- ❌ Building a site without portfolio examples

### Operations Mistakes
- ❌ Not separating business and personal finances
- ❌ Not tracking expenses/mileage
- ❌ Working without contracts
- ❌ Unclear scope or revision limits
- ❌ No clear process for handling support requests

- Salons, barbers, spas
- Restaurants and cafes
- Auto/detail shops

- Nonprofits

### Regional Advantage
Small business owners here often prefer a nearby human over a faceless agency. Use this.
