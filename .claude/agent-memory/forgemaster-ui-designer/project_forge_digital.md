The Forge Digital (theforgedigital.net) is a web design and IT support business targeting small businesses in the Farmington Valley area of Connecticut. Previously named "Root & Relay" during the naming phase.

**Why:** Jesse is building this business to serve local contractors, salons, restaurants, and professional offices with website design ($1,500-$4,000), website care ($75-$250/mo), and IT support ($75-$150/hr).

**How to apply:** The website itself serves as both the business's online presence AND a portfolio demonstration of design capability. It needs to feel authentically local and trustworthy -- like it belongs in a Connecticut mill village -- while remaining modern and professional. Brand positioning: "More than a template. Less than agency bloat."

Design evolution (March 2026):
- PREVIOUS: Dark premium aesthetic with copper/ember accent (#e07638), glassmorphic cards, floating particles
- CURRENT: Warm heritage palette reflecting Farmington Valley identity
  - Deep pine green (#2d5a3d) as primary brand color
  - Warm cream (#f5f1e8) as base background
  - Dark walnut (#4a3f35) for structure and depth
  - Muted brass (#a89668) as secondary accent
  - Libre Baskerville (serif) for headings, Inter (sans-serif) for body
  - Hero section uses mill village photo as background with dark pine-green overlay (not solid color)
  - Cards use white with warm shadow tints
  - Footer uses dark walnut
  - Local banner uses mill village photo background with dark overlay + brass accent
  - About section uses mill village photo with "Farmington Valley, CT" badge (replaced rotating SVG emblem)
  - New "Who We Serve" section between Services and Why Us, with contractor workshop photo
  - Removed: particle system, glassmorphic effects, card glow mouse tracking, rotating forge emblem SVG
  - Added: wood-grain texture hints, brass accent bars, heritage SVG logo (anvil with root/circuit lines)
  - Images stored in /images/ folder: contractor-workshop.png, mill-village-downtown.png (AI-generated, custom for brand)

Key brand signals:
- "Made right here in CT" instead of generic "Local & Responsive"
- Farmington Valley name-drops (Canton, Simsbury, Collinsville, Avon)
- Mill/forge heritage references in visual language
- Warm, neighbor-friendly feel vs. cold tech aesthetic
